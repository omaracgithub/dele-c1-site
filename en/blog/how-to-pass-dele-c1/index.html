How to Pass the DELE C1 in 4 Months

Updated: June 2025

Passing the DELE C1 is an ambitious but perfectly achievable goal with a well-structured study plan. This article provides a week-by-week guide to prepare in 4 months, starting from a solid B2 level. If you dedicate 1.5 to 2 hours daily, you'll build a strong foundation to face the exam with confidence.

Before You Start: Assess Your Level

Before diving into study, it's essential to know where you stand. Take a full DELE C1 practice exam and honestly evaluate:

  • Which tests do you score best on?
  • Where are your weak points?
  • Can you complete the tasks within the time limit?

This initial assessment will help you adjust the plan to your specific needs.

Month 1: Assessment and Vocabulary (Weeks 1-4)

Week 1: Exam Familiarization

  • Study the complete exam structure: 4 tests in 2 groups
  • Read the official instructions for each task
  • Complete a full diagnostic practice exam
  • Identify your 2-3 weakest areas

Week 2: Thematic Vocabulary I

  • Lexical fields: politics, economics, environment
  • Learn 15-20 new words daily with context
  • Practice collocations (frequent word combinations)
  • Use spaced repetition flashcards

Week 3: Thematic Vocabulary II

  • Lexical fields: technology, health, education, culture
  • Advanced discourse connectors
  • Common idiomatic expressions
  • Register differences: formal vs. informal

Week 4: Advanced Grammar

  • Subjunctive in complex subordinate clauses
  • Mixed conditional sentences
  • Reported speech in past tense
  • Advanced verbal periphrases
  • Review vocabulary from previous weeks

Month 2: Reading and Listening (Weeks 5-8)

Week 5: Reading Comprehension — Tasks 1-3

  • Practice Task 1 (global comprehension) with press articles
  • Task 2: text structure identification
  • Task 3: detailed comprehension with academic texts
  • Time each task: maximum 18 minutes per task

Week 6: Reading Comprehension — Tasks 4-5

  • Task 4: locating information across multiple texts
  • Task 5: use of language (grammar and vocabulary in context)
  • Practice time management: 90 minutes for 5 tasks
  • Analyze your errors and look for patterns

Week 7: Listening Comprehension — Tasks 1-3

  • Task 1: lectures and formal presentations
  • Task 2: conversations between multiple people
  • Task 3: interviews and debates
  • Practice note-taking during listening
  • Listen to podcasts and news in Spanish at natural speed

Week 8: Listening Comprehension — Tasks 4-5

  • Task 4: short monologues (multiple speakers)
  • Task 5: use of language in oral context
  • Practice with different accents (Spain, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia)
  • Full listening comprehension mock exam (50 minutes)

Read also: Reading Comprehension Strategies and Listening Comprehension Tips

Month 3: Writing and Speaking (Weeks 9-12)

Week 9: Written Expression — Task 1

  • Understand the format: listen to audio and write based on it
  • Practice structured note-taking
  • Text types: report, formal letter, opinion article
  • Write at least 3 complete texts this week

Week 10: Written Expression — Task 2

  • Understand the format: read a text and write based on it
  • Practice reformulation and synthesis of ideas
  • Work on textual cohesion: connectors, references, thematic progression
  • Write at least 3 complete texts this week

Week 11: Oral Expression — Preparation

  • Task 1: monologue based on a text (topic presentation)
  • Task 2: debate with the examiner
  • Task 3: negotiation based on a situation
  • Practice using the 20-minute preparation time
  • Record yourself and analyze your output

Week 12: Oral Expression — Intensive Practice

  • Simulate complete oral exams (20 min prep + 20 min)
  • Work on fluency: avoid long pauses and filler words
  • Practice transitions between ideas
  • If possible, practice with a partner or teacher

Read also: Writing Tips and Strategies

Month 4: Mock Exams and Review (Weeks 13-16)

Week 13: First Full Mock Exam

  • Complete a full exam under real time conditions
  • Group 1: Reading (90 min) + Listening (50 min)
  • Group 2: Writing (80 min) + Speaking (20+20 min)
  • Evaluate results and identify areas for improvement

Week 14: Strengthen Weak Points

  • Dedicate 70% of your time to your weakest areas
  • Review vocabulary and expressions you keep forgetting
  • Practice the specific tasks where you lose the most points
  • Work on time management if it's still an issue

Week 15: Second Full Mock Exam

  • Second complete exam under real conditions
  • Compare results with the first mock exam
  • Adjust strategies based on what you've learned
  • Practice relaxation techniques for exam day

Week 16: Final Review

  • Don't introduce new material
  • Review key vocabulary and connectors
  • Do light exercises from each test
  • Rest well in the days before the exam
  • Prepare the necessary documentation for exam day

General Tips for the 4 Months

  • Consistency over intensity: 1.5 hours every day is better than 6 hours once a week.
  • Daily immersion: Listen to podcasts, read news, and watch shows in Spanish every day.
  • Keep a log: Note new vocabulary, recurring errors, and weekly progress.
  • Practice under real conditions: Always time your exercises.
  • Don't neglect any test: Remember you need at least 30/50 in each group.
