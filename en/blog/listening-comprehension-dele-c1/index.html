DELE C1 Listening Comprehension: Tips by Task

Updated: June 2025

The listening comprehension and use of language test in the DELE C1 lasts 50 minutes and consists of 5 tasks with 30 items total. It's one of the most stressful tests because you can't control the pace: the audio moves forward and you must keep up. Here are specific tips for each task.

Test Overview

The 5 tasks assess different listening skills:

  • Task 1: Understanding main ideas and specific data in a long lecture or presentation
  • Task 2: Understanding speaker intention and attitude in conversations
  • Task 3: Detailed comprehension of an interview
  • Task 4: Identifying ideas in short monologues from different speakers
  • Task 5: Use of language in oral context (gap-filling)

Audio recordings are played twice each. Use both listenings strategically.

Task 1: Lecture or Presentation

Format: You listen to a 3-5 minute lecture, speech, or academic presentation. You answer 6 multiple-choice questions (3 options).

Specific Tips

  • Read questions beforehand: You have time before the audio to read questions. Underline key words.
  • Questions follow audio order: Don't jump between questions; go in sequence.
  • First listening: Try to answer what you can and mark uncertain ones.
  • Second listening: Confirm your answers and resolve doubts.
  • Watch for distractors: Incorrect options often use words that appear in the audio but with a different meaning.

Task 2: Conversations

Format: You listen to 4-6 short conversations between two people. For each, you must identify the speaker's intention, attitude, or feeling. Multiple choice (3 options).

Specific Tips

  • Pay attention to tone: Irony, sarcasm, and politeness are expressed through intonation.
  • Distinguish explicit from implicit: Sometimes what the speaker says isn't what they mean.
  • Notice attitude expressions: "La verdad es que...", "No es que me moleste, pero...", "Francamente..."
  • Situational context: Where are they? What's their relationship? This helps interpretation.

Task 3: Interview

Format: You listen to a 4-5 minute interview. You answer 6 multiple-choice questions about detailed information, opinions, and arguments from the interviewee.

Specific Tips

  • Identify who's speaking: Distinguish the interviewer's questions from the interviewee's answers.
  • Focus on the interviewee's opinion: Questions usually center on what the interviewee thinks or feels.
  • Watch for reformulations: The interviewee often rephrases their ideas; the second formulation is usually clearer.
  • Note specific data: Dates, names, figures that might appear in questions.

Task 4: Short Monologues

Format: You listen to 6 short monologues (30-40 seconds each) from different people. You must match each monologue with a statement from a list (there are more statements than monologues).

Specific Tips

  • Read all statements beforehand: Familiarize yourself with all available options.
  • Identify the main idea quickly: Each monologue is short; grasp the central topic in the first seconds.
  • Don't get stuck: If you're unsure about one, move to the next and come back later.
  • Watch for similar options: Two statements may seem alike but have different nuances.
  • Eliminate by process: As you assign statements, reduce options for the remaining ones.

Task 5: Use of Language (Gap-filling)

Format: You listen to an oral text and must fill in gaps with the exact missing word or expression. Tests grammar and vocabulary knowledge in oral context.

Specific Tips

  • Read the gapped text beforehand: Try to predict what type of word is missing (verb, connector, preposition).
  • First listening: Fill in the gaps you're confident about.
  • Second listening: Focus on the remaining gaps.
  • Think about grammar: If a verb is missing, what tense and mood do you need?
  • Spelling matters: Make sure you write correctly, including accent marks.

Effective Note-Taking

Note-taking is crucial in listening comprehension:

  • Use abbreviations: Develop your own system (→ for "cause", ≠ for "contrast", + for "addition")
  • Write key words, not full sentences: Don't try to transcribe
  • Organize spatially: Leave space between notes to add information during the second listening
  • Mark what you don't understand: Put a "?" to return to it during the second listening
  • Numbers and proper nouns: Always note them — they're objective data easy to verify

Dealing with Different Accents

The DELE C1 includes speakers from different Spanish-speaking countries. To prepare:

  • Listen to variety: Podcasts from Spain, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Chile
  • Don't panic: Accent differences don't prevent comprehension if you're accustomed to them
  • Regional vocabulary: Some words change (coche/carro/auto, ordenador/computadora), but context always clarifies
  • Speed: Some accents may sound faster; regular practice gets you used to it

Weekly Practice Plan

  1. Monday-Wednesday: Practice one specific task each day (20-30 min)
  2. Thursday: Extensive listening: complete podcast or radio program (30-45 min)
  3. Friday: Full listening comprehension mock exam (50 min)
  4. Weekend: Analyze errors and review new vocabulary from audio
