DELE C1 Reading Comprehension: Strategies

Updated: June 2025

The reading comprehension and use of language test is the longest in the DELE C1 exam: 90 minutes for 5 tasks and 40 items. Time management is crucial, and you need specific strategies for each task type. This article explains how to approach each one efficiently.

Time Distribution

With 90 minutes for 5 tasks, the recommended distribution is:

  • Task 1: 15-18 minutes
  • Task 2: 18-20 minutes
  • Task 3: 15-18 minutes
  • Task 4: 18-20 minutes
  • Task 5: 12-15 minutes
  • Review: 5 final minutes

Wear a watch and monitor your time. If a task is taking too long, mark your best options and move on. It's better to answer all tasks than to leave some blank.

Task 1: Global Comprehension

Format: A long text (600-700 words) from journalism, essays, or popular science. 6 multiple-choice questions (3 options) about main ideas, author's intention, and global comprehension.

Strategies

  • Read the questions first: This tells you what to look for in the text.
  • Quick first read: Identify the topic, thesis, and general structure (2-3 minutes).
  • Directed second read: Search for the specific information needed for each question.
  • Locate the evidence: For each answer, identify the paragraph or sentence that justifies it.
  • Watch for paraphrasing: The correct answer rarely uses the same words as the text; look for synonyms and reformulations.

Task 2: Text Reconstruction

Format: A text from which 6 fragments have been removed. You must place them back in the correct position. There's one extra fragment that doesn't belong (7 options for 6 gaps).

Strategies

  • Read the full text first: Understand the topic and logical progression before trying to fill gaps.
  • Look for grammatical clues: Pronouns, connectors, verb tenses that must agree.
  • Thematic coherence: The fragment must continue the previous paragraph's topic and connect to the next.
  • Start with the clearest ones: Some gaps have very obvious clues; solve those first.
  • Verify cohesion: Once you place a fragment, reread the full paragraph to confirm it flows naturally.
  • The extra fragment: Usually a distractor that covers a similar topic but doesn't fit grammatically or thematically.

Task 3: Detailed Comprehension

Format: An academic or specialized text (500-600 words). 6 multiple-choice questions about detailed information, inferences, and meaning of expressions in context.

Strategies

  • Pay attention to nuances: C1 questions test implicit comprehension, not just explicit.
  • Inference: Ask yourself "what does the author really mean?" beyond the literal words.
  • Vocabulary in context: If asked about an expression's meaning, reread the entire paragraph to grasp the sense.
  • Eliminate options: Discard clearly incorrect options before choosing among the remaining ones.
  • Don't add information: The answer must be justified by the text, not by your prior knowledge of the topic.

Task 4: Information Location

Format: Several short texts (3-6 texts) on a common theme but with different perspectives. You must identify which text contains each piece of information or idea (8-10 items).

Strategies

  • Read the statements first: Understand what information you need to locate.
  • Read each text and mark: As you read each text, note which statements might correspond to it.
  • Look for synonyms: The statements reformulate the texts' ideas with different words.
  • Opinions vs. facts: If a statement says "the author criticizes...", look for a text with a critical tone.
  • Don't confuse texts: Two texts may discuss the same subtopic but with opposing positions.

Task 5: Use of Language

Format: A text with 14 gaps. For each gap, you choose from 3 options the correct word or expression. Tests grammar, vocabulary, collocations, and fixed expressions.

Strategies

  • Read the full text first: Understand the general context before solving gaps.
  • Analyze the gap type: Is it a preposition? A connector? A collocation? A verb with prepositional government?
  • Collocations: Many items test fixed combinations (adoptar medidas, prestar atención).
  • Prepositions: Common: "depender de", "consistir en", "carecer de", "contar con".
  • Connectors: Identify the logical relationship (cause, consequence, contrast, addition) to choose correctly.

General Inference Techniques

Inference is key in the DELE C1. Practice these techniques:

  • Identify the tone: Is the author objective, critical, ironic, enthusiastic?
  • Find the implicit thesis: What does the author defend without saying it explicitly?
  • Connect ideas between paragraphs: How does each paragraph relate to the previous one?
  • Pay attention to examples: What general idea do they illustrate?
  • Attitude markers: Words like "unfortunately," "surprisingly," "as expected" reveal the author's stance.

Common Mistakes in Reading Comprehension

  • Reading too slowly: You don't need to understand every word; search for relevant information.
  • Choosing the first option that "sounds right": Always read all three options before deciding.
  • Being influenced by prior knowledge: Answer based on the text, not what you know about the topic.
  • Poor time management: Getting stuck on one task and not reaching the others.
